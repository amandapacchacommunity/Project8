How Recommendations Are Determined i Recommendations are modeled from multiple review factors rather than a single trigger.

Recommendations in this model are based on a structured review of available information. No single factor determines the result.

  • Severity of conduct: nature and impact of the behavior
  • Pattern or repetition: prior related incidents or continued conduct
  • Evidence strength: consistency, corroboration, and reliability of information
  • Policy alignment: how conduct maps to defined standards or expectations
  • Risk to community: potential for continued harm or disruption
  • Operational response need: whether training, monitoring, support measures, or formal escalation are warranted
Intake → Evidence Review → Evaluation Factors → Findings → Recommendation → Follow-Up Actions

Why this matters: Structured decision-making improves consistency, transparency, and accountability.
