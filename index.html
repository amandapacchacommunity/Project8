My Investigation Framework

A concise structure for fair, prompt, and impartial investigations.

  1. Intake and initial assessment: clarify allegation type, jurisdiction, immediate safety concerns, and reporting obligations.
  2. Investigation planning: identify parties, witnesses, evidence sources, and documentation needs.
  3. Neutral fact gathering: conduct thorough interviews and collect records, digital evidence, and relevant contextual information.
  4. Trauma-informed process management: maintain fairness while using communication practices that reduce unnecessary harm.
  5. Analysis and findings: evaluate credibility, consistency, corroboration, and policy application.
  6. Report and hearing support: present findings clearly, maintain complete records, and support institutional decision-making.
